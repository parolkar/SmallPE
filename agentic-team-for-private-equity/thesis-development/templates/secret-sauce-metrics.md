# Secret Sauce Metrics Framework

## Overview

Your "secret sauce" is the most critical component of your fund thesis - it's what sets you apart from other firms and convinces LPs of your unique value proposition. This must be **quantifiable and credible**, not vague claims.

## The Seven Credibility Metrics (In Priority Order)

LPs evaluate your differentiation using these metrics, ranked by importance:

### 1. Investment Exits ðŸ¥‡
**Highest credibility signal**

**What to quantify:**
- Number of successful exits
- Total exit value
- Types of exits (acquisition, IPO, secondary)
- Multiples achieved (if strong)

**Examples:**
- "15+ successful exits totaling $3.5B"
- "Track record of 8 exits in sustainable technology"
- "3 exits averaging 8.2x MOIC"

**When to use:**
- You have direct investment experience with realized exits
- Exits are relevant to your target sector/stage
- Exit values are material and verifiable

**Common pitfalls:**
- Claiming credit for exits you didn't lead
- Including marginal or token advisory roles
- Counting unrealized "paper exits"

---

### 2. Investment Performance ðŸ¥ˆ
**Strong credibility signal**

**What to quantify:**
- Documented ROI or IRR
- MOIC (Multiple on Invested Capital)
- DPI (Distributed to Paid-In) if applicable
- Consistent top-quartile performance

**Examples:**
- "Portfolio generating 35% IRR over 7 years"
- "Track record of 4.2x average MOIC across 12 investments"
- "Consistent top-decile performance in [sector]"

**When to use:**
- You have verifiable investment track record
- Performance is strong relative to benchmarks
- Time period is material (3+ years)

**Common pitfalls:**
- Cherry-picking best performers
- Short track records with limited data
- Unrealized valuations as "performance"
- Comparing incomparable vintages

---

### 3. Capital Raised ðŸ¥‰
**Moderate credibility signal**

**What to quantify:**
- Total funding secured for portfolio companies
- Number of financing rounds led/supported
- Follow-on capital attraction rate

**Examples:**
- "Helped portfolio companies raise $500MM+ in follow-on capital"
- "Led or co-led 23 financing rounds totaling $180MM"
- "100% of portfolio secured Series A within 18 months"

**When to use:**
- You have strong fundraising track record
- Relevant to your value proposition (e.g., seed fund helping companies reach Series A)
- Demonstrates network and credibility with other investors

**Common pitfalls:**
- Claiming credit for capital you didn't help secure
- Conflating your fund size with portfolio capital raised
- Including capital raised before your involvement

---

### 4. Sales Closed
**Moderate credibility for commercial expertise**

**What to quantify:**
- Revenue generated in operating roles
- Deals closed (number and value)
- Customer acquisition track record

**Examples:**
- "Generated $50MM in enterprise sales over 5 years at [Company]"
- "Closed 200+ deals averaging $250K ACV"
- "Built sales team from 0 to $20MM ARR in 3 years"

**When to use:**
- You have strong commercial operating background
- Relevant to your value proposition (e.g., helping portfolio companies with go-to-market)
- Demonstrates ability to support portfolio operationally

**Common pitfalls:**
- Team sales numbers claimed as individual achievement
- Irrelevant sales experience (B2C when targeting B2B)
- Inflated or unverifiable numbers

---

### 5. Companies Helped
**Baseline credibility signal**

**What to quantify:**
- Number of startups/companies supported
- Types of support provided (advisory, investment, operational)
- Success rate of companies helped

**Examples:**
- "Advised 40+ early-stage SaaS companies"
- "Supported 15 companies through scaling from seed to Series B"
- "Mentored 50+ founders in accelerator programs"

**When to use:**
- You lack investment exits but have advisory experience
- Relevant to demonstrating pattern recognition
- Shows commitment to founder support

**Common pitfalls:**
- Vague "consulting" without specific outcomes
- Counting every coffee meeting as "helping"
- No evidence of meaningful impact

---

### 6. Network Size
**Supporting credibility signal**

**What to quantify:**
- Size of relevant network (LPs, founders, domain experts, customers)
- Specificity of network (job titles, companies)
- Access advantages (university, corporate, geographic)

**Examples:**
- "Network of 2,000+ enterprise tech founders"
- "Database of 500+ AI scientists and researchers"
- "Direct relationships with 300+ corporate innovation heads"
- "Access to 1,000+ MIT/Harvard technical founders"

**When to use:**
- Network provides deal flow advantage
- Network supports portfolio (customers, hires, partnerships)
- Network is verifiable and specific

**Common pitfalls:**
- LinkedIn connection count as "network"
- Unquantified "extensive network" claims
- Irrelevant network (consumer when targeting B2B)

---

### 7. Years of Experience
**Baseline credibility signal**

**What to quantify:**
- Years in relevant industry/sector
- Years in relevant role (operator, investor, advisor)
- Specific domain expertise depth

**Examples:**
- "15 years leading fintech startups"
- "12-year tenure in enterprise infrastructure sales"
- "20 years investing in healthcare technology"

**When to use:**
- You have deep sector expertise
- Combined with other metrics for credibility
- Demonstrates pattern recognition and market knowledge

**Common pitfalls:**
- Years of experience without relevant achievements
- Counting tangentially related experience
- Leading with years when stronger metrics available

### Sector-Specific Guidance

**For Technology/Software:**
- Emphasize: Technical expertise, operator experience, exits
- Example: "...with PhD in quantum physics and experience commercializing 3 university patents"

**For Impact/Climate:**
- Emphasize: Sector exits, industry network, operational success
- Example: "...track record of 8 exits in sustainable technology and connections to industrial partners"

**For B2B/Enterprise:**
- Emphasize: Commercial success, customer network, operational scaling
- Example: "...experience scaling 3 SaaS startups to $10MM+ ARR and network of 500+ enterprise customers"

**For Lower-Middle Market PE:**
- Emphasize: Operational value creation, exits, industry expertise
- Example: "...track record of 12 successful platform exits and 20 years in manufacturing operations"

## Verification and Credibility

### Make It Defensible

Every metric should be:
1. **Verifiable**: Can be confirmed through public records or references
2. **Specific**: Exact numbers, not ranges or vague claims
3. **Relevant**: Directly related to your investment strategy
4. **Recent**: Ideally within last 5-10 years (unless legendary track record)

### Red Flags That Destroy Credibility

âŒ "Decades of experience across multiple industries"

## Testing Your Secret Sauce

Ask yourself:
1. **Could a competitor claim this?** If yes, make it more specific
2. **Can you prove it?** If no, soften the claim or provide evidence
3. **Does it matter to LPs?** If no, choose a more relevant metric
4. **Is it authentic to you?** If no, find what truly differentiates you
